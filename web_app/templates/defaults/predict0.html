Let's talk product

If organizations such as the IRA in Russia are truly guilty of medling in the 2016 U.S Presidential Election, the ads put out to American computers, phones, and tablets, are of great interest to us. There is a great chance that any given person could have been targeted by these ads, but on what basis. The advertisements are not targeted to only one or even a few interest groups, but many different ones. Let's takes a look at a breadown of some metrics of these advertisements; such as the number of ads targeted to each interest group, the amount of clicks that these advertisements got, and how much money was spent on advertising.

For the Motherland




Number of Ads in Interest Groups

As we can see from out sample, the vast majority of the ads were targeted to African Americans, Progressives, and Latin Americans. Many of the ads were targeted to multiple groups at once, especially African Americans and Progressives.



Number of Clicks per Interest Group



Of all the advertisements that were targeted to a particular interest group, this is the aggregate of the combined clicks among all the ads. As there were a higher volume of ads targeted towards African Americans, Progressives, and Latin Americans, it is unsurprising that these ads registered the highest number of clicks.





Interest Group Spending

This is the amount of money that was spent on advertisements for each interest group. African American and Progressive focused ads make up the two largest areas of spending, and Latin American-focused ads make up a relatively small portion of the spending.
